# First Atomic Proof Options v1

## A first step into Post Fiat

Post Fiat is an independent XRPL-derived Layer 1 network.

It began as an XRP hard fork, but it is not an XRP token, XRP app, or feature inside XRP. Post Fiat is its own L1 and XRP competitor focused on verified human work, AI-assisted coordination, portable reputation, and contribution-based rewards.

This page is not a full onboarding guide.

It is a simple menu of three small Post Fiat-native proof actions that a newcomer can complete in under 30 minutes.

Pick one option. Submit one proof-backed response.

No wallet, account, or private information is required to complete this first proof.

---

## Option 1: Clarity Signal

**Time:** 10–20 minutes  
**Best for:** Newcomers who found something that made Post Fiat click.

Review one public Post Fiat page and identify one section that made Post Fiat easier to understand.

Your response should answer:

- What page did you review?
- What section, sentence, or idea helped most?
- What did it clarify?
- Why would this help another newcomer?

**Proof expectation:**

Submit:

- The public URL you reviewed
- A screenshot or copied excerpt of the section that helped
- A short explanation of why it made Post Fiat clearer

---

## Option 2: Friction Signal

**Time:** 10–20 minutes  
**Best for:** Newcomers who found something confusing, vague, or hard to act on.

Review one public Post Fiat page and identify one section that slowed you down.

Your response should answer:

- What page did you review?
- What section, sentence, or idea felt unclear?
- What did you think it meant?
- What was confusing?
- What small change would make it clearer?

**Proof expectation:**

Submit:

- The public URL you reviewed
- A screenshot or copied excerpt of the section that caused friction
- A short explanation of the confusion
- One suggested improvement

---

## Option 3: Discovery Signal

**Time:** 10–20 minutes  
**Best for:** Newcomers who want to explain what brought them into Post Fiat.

Describe how you first found Post Fiat, what made you curious enough to take the next step, and what signal helped the network feel worth exploring.

Your response should answer:

- How did you first find Post Fiat?
- What made you pay attention?
- What made you take the next step?
- What still feels unclear?
- If you are aware of GoodAlexander, how did his presence, writing, posting, or explanations affect your interest?
- If you are not aware of GoodAlexander, that is completely fine. Just say so.

**Proof expectation:**

Submit:

- A short written response
- Any public link, post, page, screenshot, or artifact that helped bring you in
- One sentence explaining what made you move from passive interest to active exploration

---

## Privacy rule

Use only public or privacy-safe references. Do not include private chats, personal account details, wallet information, private DMs, or non-public screenshots.

---

## Simple submission format

Copy and fill out one option.

```txt
Selected option:
Public page, post, or artifact reviewed:
What I noticed:
Why it mattered:
What made me more interested, if applicable:
What still feels unclear, if applicable:
Proof attached:
Suggested improvement, if any:
Privacy-safe redactions used, if any:
```

---

## What counts as a good first atomic proof?

A good first proof is small, specific, and easy to verify.

It should show:

- One clear observation
- One public or privacy-safe reference
- One screenshot, copied excerpt, link, or written response
- One short explanation
- One reason the observation helps Post Fiat understand newcomer interest

The goal is not to explain all of Post Fiat.

The goal is to turn one honest newcomer reaction into useful proof.
